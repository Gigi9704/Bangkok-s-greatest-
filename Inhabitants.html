POPULATION

Bangkok, the capital of Thailand, has been a residence for numerous notable figures across diverse domains. Here are some well-known residents associated with Bangkok:


Christina Aguilar


Christina Aguilar was born in Bangkok on 31 October 1966. She is a Thai singer known as the Thai Queen of Dance. She
is the first and only Thai artist to have their first four studio albums sell over 1 million
copies; her third album Red Beat is the best-selling female artist’s album of
all time in Thai music industry, selling 3 million copies.


Mario Maurer


Mario Maurer was born on 4 December 1988 in Bangkok, Thailand. He is a Thai actor and model who was the lead star of Thailand’s
highest grossing film of all time, Pee Mak alongside Davika Hoorne.

Maurer achieved acclaim for his performances in the 2007 movie "The Love of Siam" and the 2010 surprise success
"Crazy Little Thing Called Love." He secured the Best Actor accolade at the Starpics Thai Films Awards and
received nominations from both the Bangkok Critics Assembly and Star Entertainment Awards.


Sirivannavari Nariratana


Princess Sirivannavari Nariratana Rajakanya was born on 8th January 1987 in Bangkok. She holds the title of Princess
in the Kingdom of Thailand and is the solitary daughter of King Vajiralongkorn within the line of succession, possessing royal
designations. Initially named Mom Chao Busyanambejra Mahidol at birth, she subsequently altered
her name to Mom Chao Chakkrityapha Mahidol and later once again to Mom Chao
Sirivanvari Mahidol under the directive of Queen Sirikit. Aside from her royal status, Sirivannavari
Nariratana has demonstrated her talents in multiple domains. She has showcased her
abilities as a fashion designer, creating an outfit worn by the representative from Thailand in the
Miss Universe Thailand 2018 competition. Furthermore,
she has actively participated in sports, engaging as an accomplished
equestrian and a former badminton player.


Tammy Duckworth


Born on March 12, 1968, in Bangkok, Thailand, Ladda Tammy Duckworth is a Thai American politician. She served as a
representative for Illinois's 8th congressional district in the U.S. House of Representatives from
2013 to 2017. Additionally, a former Army National Guard lieutenant colonel,
she now holds the position of junior United States
senator from Illinois since 2017.

The second daughter of King Bhumibol, Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn is actively involved in philanthropy and cultural endeavors. She is particularly known for her dedication to education and developmental initiatives.


Tony Jaa
A martial artist and actor, Tony Jaa gained global recognition for his roles in action films like "Ong-Bak: Muay Thai Warrior." While born in Surin Province, he spent much of his career in Bangkok.


Jim Thompson
An American entrepreneur and art collector, Jim Thompson significantly contributed to promoting Thai silk internationally. He resided in Bangkok, and his enigmatic disappearance in the Cameron Highlands is a well-known narrative.


Chalermchai Kositpipat
A contemporary Thai artist, Chalermchai Kositpipat is renowned for his work on the White Temple (Wat Rong Khun) in Chiang Rai. His origins are in Bangkok.
